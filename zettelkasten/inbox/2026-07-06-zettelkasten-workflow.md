# Building a Zettelkasten System

## What caught my attention?
The need for a structured knowledge management system that bridges between:
- Daily research reading (papers, blogs, podcasts)
- Fleeting thoughts and questions
- Permanent, distilled insights
- Blog publishing (Hugo)

## Why does this matter to me?
As a PhD student working at the intersection of RL and LLMs, I consume a lot of frontier research. Without a system, insights evaporate. The Zettelkasten forces active processing — not just collecting.

## Raw thoughts
- The key insight is that **questions are as valuable as answers**, especially at the frontier where nobody has the answers yet
- Confidence levels (high/medium/low/frontier) are crucial for honest epistemic tracking
- The `frontier` confidence level is special — it marks where AI can't help with knowledge, only with scaffolding questions
- Blog posts should emerge from clusters of permanent notes, not be written from scratch
- The Hermes Agent can serve as a "thinking partner" that helps connect notes and surface patterns
